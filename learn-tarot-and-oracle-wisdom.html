History of Cartomancy
Cartomancy, the practice of using cards to divine insight, dates back centuries, blending mysticism, intuition, and art. From early playing cards in the 14th century to the intricate tarot decks we use today, cartomancy has long served as a bridge between the physical and the spiritual worlds. Originally viewed as a simple parlor game, the deeper symbolism of these cards was soon recognized for its potential to reveal subconscious truths and divine guidance.

Tarot, with its 78 cards and rich symbology, became a cornerstone of cartomancy, while oracle decks—with their open-ended, intuitive messages—have risen in popularity for those seeking more personal, flexible guidance. Both forms of divination allow practitioners to tap into higher wisdom, explore emotions, and uncover paths forward in life.

Exploring practices
At its heart, cartomancy is about connection—to ourselves, to our intuition, and to the universe. Whether you prefer the structured journey of a tarot reading or the free-flowing energy of an oracle spread, cartomancy offers endless possibilities for insight and growth.

Common practices include daily draws for reflection, spreads for deeper understanding of relationships or career paths, and readings focused on spiritual development. No matter your practice, what’s important is that the cards serve as a mirror for your inner world, helping you to navigate life with greater clarity and awareness.

Through guided lessons, assignments, and intuitive exercises, you'll learn how to harness the power of your own deck, tailoring your readings to suit your needs and desires. Whether it’s tarot or oracle, these tools will help you unfold the story of your life, one card at a time.
